Our Mission Statement:

To provide cost-effective R&D solutions for science, engineering, and technology.

    L-Egant Solutions (LES) sells high-voltage components, systems, and diagnostics; products that are focussed on research and development activities involving the delivery of high-electrical power, commonly referred to as “pulsed-power.” This speciality relies on the use of sophisticated, high-voltage equipment, for charging, storing, switching, and delivering electrical energy efficiently, and often at the fastest timescales possible to produce extremes in the assembled energy density. Frequently, the materials involved are in a state of high-electrical ionization, that is characteristic of the field of physics that studies plasma.

    As the founding principal of LES and an active research physicist/scientist, I am happy to extol my many years experience in these areas. I founded this company to address many years of difficulties I experienced in finding cost-effective laboratory equipment for my own research. It often seemed that my needs were always a little more expensive than the resource funding that I had available. Thus, I usually had to resort to building something from scratch, or by modifying surplus components, rather than buying it outright. Of course, this is a gratifying learning process of discovery. However, it does negatively impact your goal-focused workflow and project productivity.

    My career in research started with a Ph.D. in physics, from the University of California, Irvine, studying methods to inject intense ion beams into a fusion reactor. After that I worked on ion-space propulsion at the Hughes Research Laboratories, where I was fortunate enough to be close to UCLA and where I could regularly attend their seminar series on entrepreneurship, at a time when startups did not have the same appeal as they do today. While still at HRL, my colleagues and I embarked on writing our own business plan and succeeded at funding MicroBeam, Inc., developing focused-ion beams for microelectronics at $1 M/unit. Shortly after opening our doors, I left the company, as it was necessary to be “more” optimistic about our sales than what reality could support.

    I returned to UC Irvine, doing R & D on magnetic-field compression in a Z-pinch, x-ray lithography, and intense-charged particle beam propagation. Z-pinches have historically been an attractive option for fusion, since they provide an easy way to produce extremes in the compressed-plasma-energy density. Eventually, I went on to successfully fund the "Staged Z-pinch" (SZP) Project, in a highly competitive procurement for alternate fusion concepts from the Department of Energy.

    Ultimately, the SZP lead to a new method for energy delivery in a fusion target, with many papers and two patents that eventually served as the basis for my third start-up 15 years later, Magneto-Inertial Fusion Technologies, Inc. (MIFTI), which continues to develop the SZP for fusion and the production of medical isotopes.

    My earlier PhD work with Norman Rostoker, on intense-beam propagation also evolved into another new concept for fusion, based on a Field-Reversed Magnetic Configuration (FRC). One day, while I was chatting with Norman about the lack of funding for this idea, I suggested we write a business plan and raise money, which we eventually did, with others, to form my second startup, Tri Alpha Energy.

    Laboratory based R&D is a highly-competitive, cost-constrained endeavor, typical in all disciplines of science, engineering and technology. Today, I am less focused on developing new fusion ideas, and more on providing cost-effective products, solutions, and diagnostics to researchers; some of which are compiled here on the L-Egant Solutions website. Our high voltage component offerings are imported from Zeonics Systech, of India, founded in 1981. Zeonics is a leader in high-voltage, pulsed-power components and systems and many of their products perform similarly to what is available in the USA, at substantially reduced cost over comparable items available domestically.

    LES is also offering our first, home-grown diagnostic product, the - Second-Harmonic Dispersion Interferometer (SHDI). The SHDI is available in two configurations: as a 1-dimensional probe instrument and a 2-dimensional probe instrument. Having used this diagnostic in my personal research, I am convinced that it will soon be in high demand for many applications, from plasma, gas, and liquids, especially for microscopy. For those of you interested in developing/offering your own diagnostic products, please don't hesitate to reach out to me.

    As always, it will be a great pleasure to provide you with the highest level of support for your research needs, and to facilitate your discoveries efficiently, at low cost.
